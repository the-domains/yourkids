Toddlers Online?

Brian O’Neill from the Dublin Institute of Technology gave an interesting presentation last year to folk interested in the Digital health of our children and entitled it ‘Toddlers and Tech'. It strikes me that increasingly we’re discussing kids online at a younger and younger age.

I created the above graphic in 2016, and clearly need to update it. However, it’s not just the fact that they go online. Our kids are online. That’s their experience now - whether we give them a phone or not. Recently I overheard my daughter, who was 6 at the time, shouting at the Radio in my bedroom: “Alexa, stop!”. It didn’t occur to her to press the off button, she expected the 7 year old radio player to understand what she was saying and act accordingly.

We’re still trying to understand what the effect this new online world is having on our brains, on our social experience, and on our politics. But perhaps the biggest question parents should be asked is: how is this effecting your kids?
