Google’s losing battle

After a Medium article - ref - there is an increasing interest in videos that pretend to be family-friendly only to reveal adult content when viewed in full

As a parent, this is a frustrating battle. I knew that “YouTube Kids” wouldn’t be a guarantee of safety. However, now that this is news, it also becomes a self-fulfiling prophesy. By challenging this, and saying they will get tough on such abuses, Google has laid down the gauntlet. It’s now a challenge for online denizens to devise ways they can trick Google’s algorithms to smuggle in more content into walled-garden areas.

In recent months, we've noticed a growing trend around content on YouTube that attempts to pass as family-friendly, but is clearly not. - Google Blog November 22, 2017

Google is in a difficult position. They need to respond to the threat, and the media interest forces them to do so publically. However, they are now fighting a battle with an internet desperate to subvert what they do.

The other issue, highlighted by the London Times is the co-opting of innocent-meaning videos by children by predators.

“Some of the world’s biggest brands are advertising on YouTube videos showing scantily clad children that have attracted comments from hundreds of paedophiles.” - Alexi Mostrous, The Times

This is easier for Google to combat - they can automatically reduce comments for example. However, they could do more. They could encourage children creating videos to limit the audience to their friends and family.
